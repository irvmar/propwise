# PropWise AI -- NARPM Outreach Strategy

---

## 1. Overview

NARPM (National Association of Residential Property Managers) is the primary professional organization for residential property managers in the United States, with 6,000+ members across 100+ local chapters. It represents our exact ICP (Ideal Customer Profile): professional property managers who operate portfolios of 50-500+ units and are actively seeking tools to improve efficiency and professionalism.

This document outlines a multi-channel strategy for establishing PropWise AI as a trusted technology partner within the NARPM ecosystem.

---

## 2. Understanding the NARPM Landscape

### Membership Profile
- **Company Size:** Primarily small to mid-size PM firms (50-300 units typical)
- **Decision Makers:** Company owners, operations directors, maintenance coordinators
- **Technology Adoption:** Varies widely -- many still use spreadsheets and personal phones; the progressive segment actively seeks automation
- **Key Pain Points:** Staffing shortages, after-hours coverage, scaling without proportional headcount, tenant communication volume, vendor coordination

### NARPM Structure
- **National Board:** Sets standards, hosts the annual conference (NARPM National Convention & Trade Show)
- **Regional Vice Presidents (RVPs):** Oversee clusters of chapters
- **Local Chapters:** Monthly meetings, local education, networking events
- **Designations:** RMP (Residential Management Professional), MPM (Master Property Manager), CRMC (Certified Residential Management Company) -- members pursuing these are invested in professional growth and are strong prospects

## 3. Local Chapter Approach

### Phase 1: Research & Relationship Building (Months 1-2)

1. **Identify Priority Chapters**
   - Target 10-15 chapters in markets where PropWise already has customers or strong product-market fit
   - Prioritize chapters in high-growth Sun Belt markets: Phoenix, Dallas-Fort Worth, Atlanta, Tampa, Charlotte, Nashville, Austin, Raleigh, Jacksonville, Denver
   - Research each chapter's leadership (President, VP of Education, VP of Membership)

2. **Join as an Affiliate Member**
   - NARPM offers Affiliate Membership for vendors/service providers (~$350/year)
   - This grants access to member directories, chapter events, and the national conference trade show
   - Membership signals legitimacy and long-term commitment to the industry

3. **Attend 2-3 Chapter Meetings as a Guest**
   - Do NOT pitch at the first meeting. Listen, learn, build rapport.
   - Ask questions about their biggest operational challenges
   - Take notes on the language they use to describe problems (mirror this in our marketing)
   - Offer genuine value: "I work in property management technology -- happy to help if anyone has questions about AI or automation"

### Phase 2: Education-First Engagement (Months 2-4)

4. **Offer Free Educational Presentations to Chapters**
   - Approach the VP of Education or Chapter President
   - Position as vendor-neutral education, not a sales pitch
   - Suggested presentation topics (see Section 6 below)
   - Provide CE credits if possible (coordinate with NARPM national for approval)
   - Typical format: 30-45 minute presentation + 15 minute Q&A at a monthly meeting

5. **Provide Chapter-Specific Value**
   - Create a free resource: "The Property Manager's Guide to AI in 2026" (PDF)
   - Offer to host a free "Technology Office Hours" session for chapter members
   - Sponsor a chapter lunch or happy hour ($200-500, high ROI for relationship building)

### Phase 3: Conversion & Expansion (Months 4-8)

6. **Pilot Program for Chapter Members**
   - Offer NARPM members an exclusive extended trial (30 days instead of 14)
   - Provide a NARPM-specific discount: 15% off the first year
   - Create case studies from early adopters within the chapter

7. **Chapter Sponsor/Partner Status**
   - Many chapters accept local sponsors ($500-2,000/year depending on market)
   - Benefits typically include: logo on chapter website, mention in monthly emails, table at meetings, speaking opportunities
   - Target 5-8 chapter sponsorships in Year 1

8. **Leverage Success for Expansion**
   - Once established in 3-5 chapters, approach the Regional VP to present at regional events
   - Use testimonials from NARPM members in all subsequent chapter outreach
   - Ask satisfied NARPM customers for warm introductions to other chapter leaders

## 4. National Conference Strategy

### NARPM Annual Convention & Trade Show

The NARPM National Convention is the premier annual event, typically held in October, drawing 1,500-2,000 attendees. This is the single highest-ROI event for PropWise.

#### Pre-Conference (8-12 Weeks Before)

- **Secure a Trade Show Booth**
  - Standard booth: ~$2,500-4,000
  - Premium/corner placement: ~$5,000-7,000
  - Budget for booth design, materials, giveaways: $3,000-5,000
  - Total conference investment: $8,000-15,000

- **Pre-Schedule Meetings**
  - Email all existing NARPM contacts to schedule booth visits or coffee meetings
  - Reach out to 50 target companies with personalized invitations to visit the booth
  - Offer a "conference special" pricing tier (creates urgency)

- **Submit Speaking Proposals**
  - NARPM accepts speaker proposals 6-9 months in advance
  - Submit 2-3 proposals (see Section 6 for topics)
  - If not selected as a speaker, offer to co-present with an existing customer

#### During the Conference

- **Booth Strategy**
  - Live demo stations: let attendees text PropWise and see the AI respond in real-time
  - "Text us your worst maintenance request" interactive challenge -- attendees text a demo number and see how PropWise handles it
  - Lead capture: tablet with quick qualification questions (portfolio size, current tools, biggest pain point)
  - Giveaway: branded item relevant to PMs (quality phone charger with "PropWise: So your phone can die, but your tenant communication won't")

- **Networking Events**
  - Attend all social events (welcome reception, awards dinner, after-parties)
  - Host a small invite-only dinner for 15-20 high-value prospects and existing customers (budget: $2,000-3,000)
  - Be present, be helpful, don't hard-sell in social settings

- **Content Capture**
  - Record video testimonials from happy customers at the booth
  - Take photos for social media (tag NARPM and attendees)
  - Live-post key takeaways from sessions to LinkedIn/Twitter

#### Post-Conference (1-4 Weeks After)

- **Follow-Up Sequence**
  - Day 1-2: Personal email to everyone who visited the booth ("Great meeting you at NARPM...")
  - Day 3-5: Send the promised demo link or resource
  - Day 7-10: Follow up with a case study relevant to their portfolio size
  - Day 14-21: Direct ask for a demo call
  - Track all leads in CRM with "NARPM Conference 2026" tag

## 6. Local Chapter Presentation Topics

These topics are designed to be educational first, with PropWise positioned as a natural example -- not a sales pitch. Each should be 30-45 minutes with Q&A.

### Topic 1: "AI for Property Managers: What's Real, What's Hype, and What to Do Now"
- **Audience:** PM company owners, operations managers
- **Outline:**
  - The current state of AI in property management (honest assessment)
  - What AI can realistically automate today vs. what still needs humans
  - Three areas where AI delivers immediate ROI for PMs: tenant communication, maintenance triage, rent collection follow-up
  - Live demo: show how an AI agent handles a real tenant conversation
  - Framework for evaluating AI tools (not just PropWise -- give genuine buying criteria)
  - Q&A

### Topic 2: "Scaling Your Portfolio Without Scaling Your Payroll"
- **Audience:** Growth-stage PM companies (100-500 units)
- **Outline:**
  - The staffing math problem: why hiring linearly kills margins
  - Industry benchmarks: units-per-employee ratios and how top performers differ
  - The three leverage points: communication automation, maintenance workflow, financial automation
  - Case study: How a 200-unit PM company grew to 350 units with the same team
  - Technology stack recommendations (PM software + AI + communication tools)
  - Q&A

### Topic 3: "After-Hours Management: Stop Losing Sleep and Start Losing Liability"
- **Audience:** Any PM company handling after-hours calls
- **Outline:**
  - The real cost of after-hours calls: staff burnout, turnover, liability exposure, missed emergencies
  - What qualifies as a true emergency vs. what can wait (decision framework)
  - Comparison of solutions: answering services vs. on-call rotations vs. AI triage
  - How AI-powered triage works: emergency detection, automatic escalation, tenant reassurance
  - Setting up an after-hours protocol that protects your team and your tenants
  - Q&A

### Topic 4: "The Tenant Communication Playbook: From Move-In to Move-Out"
- **Audience:** PM companies focused on tenant retention
- **Outline:**
  - Why communication is the #1 driver of lease renewals (data and research)
  - The tenant communication lifecycle: pre-move-in, onboarding, ongoing, renewal, move-out
  - Channel preferences by tenant demographic (SMS vs. email vs. portal vs. phone)
  - Automating the routine so you can personalize what matters
  - Templates and scripts for the 10 most common tenant interactions
  - Q&A

### Topic 5: "Maintenance Coordination in 2026: Systems That Actually Work"
- **Audience:** Maintenance coordinators, operations managers
- **Outline:**
  - Why maintenance is the most time-consuming function in PM (survey data)
  - The maintenance communication triangle: tenant, PM, vendor -- and where things break down
  - Building a triage system: categorization, prioritization, and routing
  - Technology tools for maintenance automation (work order systems, AI triage, vendor platforms)
  - Measuring maintenance performance: response time, resolution time, tenant satisfaction, cost per work order
  - Q&A
